\chapter{Conclusion}
\label{Chapter5}

This dissertation reassessed 56 \emph{health economics} studies under a common replication and re-estimation protocol to examine how results change when TWFE is replaced by modern DiD estimators. The literature remains broadly recognizable, but many conclusions become less secure. In this sample, changes rarely take the form of sign reversal. They appear more often as changes in magnitude, weaker dynamic profiles, loss of precision, and greater sensitivity to design choices than the original articles suggested.

The contribution of this dissertation is empirical and comparative. Re-estimating a defined applied literature under a common protocol makes it possible to distinguish between results that remain substantively stable and results that weaken once identification choices are made explicit. It also sharpens how the recent critique of TWFE should be read in practice. In this sample, divergence is not driven mainly by negative weights. It more often reflects differences in control-group choice, support, dynamic aggregation, and the treatment of covariates. For applied work, the relevant question is whether the article's conclusion survives once those margins are made explicit under alternative estimators.

The exercise has clear limits. The sample is restricted to studies with usable replication materials and does not represent the full DiD literature. The analysis uses the data and code environments provided by the original authors and may therefore inherit limitations from those replication packages. The sample is concentrated in \emph{health economics}, with many studies set in the United States, which limits generalization across fields and institutional settings. Some replications also required adaptations to make the original specifications compatible with a standardized implementation in \emph{R}. The scope of the dissertation is therefore specific. It provides an auditable and comparable reassessment of a defined sample.

These limits also point to natural extensions. The same protocol can be applied to literatures outside \emph{health economics}, to settings with different data structures, and to designs outside the present sample, including \emph{triple-DiD} and non-absorbing treatments.

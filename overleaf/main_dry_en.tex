\begin{abstract}
\addchaptertocentry{\abstractname}

Two-way fixed-effects (TWFE) difference-in-differences (DiD) has been the default
estimator in applied health-policy research for decades, yet a rapidly growing
methodological literature has shown that TWFE can implicitly weight cohort--time
effects in ways that conflict with the causal parameter the analyst has in mind.
This dissertation asks whether the conclusions of 56 published health-economics
papers that used TWFE survive reestimation with modern heterogeneity-robust
estimators: Callaway--Sant'Anna (CS-DID), Sun--Abraham (SA),
Borusyak--Jaravel--Spiess (BJS), Goodman-Bacon's decomposition, and the
Rambachan--Roth (HonestDiD) sensitivity framework.

The exercise follows each paper's own preferred specification and replaces only
the estimator, so any divergence is attributable to methodology rather than to
redesign. Across the 53 comparable articles, the sign of the effect is preserved in 47 cases
and reverses in 6. The more interesting changes happen on
other margins. Standard errors rise by a median of 36\% under
heterogeneity-robust estimation, 14 articles lose conventional statistical
significance, and the magnitude of the point estimate moves by more than 50\% in
32\% of designs (21\% move by more than 100\%). Bacon decompositions show that
treated-versus-treated comparisons account for a substantial share of the
identifying variation in staggered TWFE specifications, which is consistent with
the mechanism emphasized by the theory literature. HonestDiD sensitivity
exercises further show that robustness to parallel-trends violations is limited
under both TWFE and CS-DID.

The contribution is threefold. First, I construct a reproducible metadata-driven
pipeline that re-estimates each paper from its original replication package with
a fixed random seed, making every bootstrap-dependent quantity bit-identical
across runs. Second, the comparative evidence shows that the practical cost of
legacy TWFE designs in health economics is concentrated in precision and
magnitude rather than in directional conclusions. Third, the package itself
(released under CC-BY-4.0 and compliant with the AEA Data and Code Availability
Policy 1.0) provides a template for future methodological audits of applied
literatures.

\end{abstract}
